Main timeline

From pioneer engines to modern icons.

The timeline organises the major turning points of the automobile and connects them to Moroccan landmarks: the first Daimler delivered to Sultan Hassan I in 1892, the Rabat-Casablanca race in 1912, the Rallye du Maroc in 1926 and the Casablanca Grand Prix in 1958.

1886-1910Birth of the automobile 1910-1930The age of pioneers 1930-1945Design, speed and prestige 1945-1959Rebirth and grand touring 1960-1969Modern icons before 1970
Key moments

The dates that changed the road and Morocco's motoring memory.

Each milestone becomes a mediation tool: technical invention, social use, a link to the Museum collection and a possible continuation in the Media Room.

1886
Birth

Benz Patent-Motorwagen

Carl Benz's patent marks the transition from mechanical experimentation to the automobile as an autonomous object.

1908
Democratisation

Ford Model T

Mass production turns the car into a social phenomenon and permanently transforms mobility.

1912
Morocco

First Rabat-Casablanca race

A roughly 90 km route placed Morocco very early in the sporting and road history of the automobile.

1926
Rally

First edition of the Rallye du Maroc

The competition becomes a national narrative of roads, endurance and mechanical mastery.

1955
Futuristic design

Citroën DS

The DS imposes an almost architectural vision of the car: comfort, aerodynamics and modernity.

1962
Grand touring

1962 collector roadster

German engineering meets a sculptural presence that has become a collection icon.

1961
Cult sports car

Gallery of Museum classics

A long, low, unmistakable line: the sports car becomes a cultural icon.

1962
Italian luxury

Red collector Fiat

Italian grand touring becomes a language of rarity, elegance and performance.

1964
German elegance

Mercedes-Benz Pagoda

The Pagoda embodies a discreet, powerful and instantly recognisable silhouette.

1965
Popular culture

American coupe

The American coupe opens up a new mythology: accessible, expressive and energetic.

Morocco focus

The automobile as a culture of transmission.

In Morocco, heritage motoring meets roads, gatherings, collectors, workshops and the urban elegance of Casablanca. The Museum turns this heritage into a living place: museum, collection, dining, academy and events.
